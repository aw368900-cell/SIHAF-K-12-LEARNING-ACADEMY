Who We Are

A Community Built on Education & Hope

Founded in Shreveport, Louisiana, SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy began as a small after-school program serving a handful of students in the heart of the city. Today, we have grown into a full-service nonprofit educational organization supporting K-12 students across the Shreveport-Bossier metro area.

We believe every child deserves access to high-quality educational resources, positive role models, and a safe, nurturing environment where they can thrive academically and personally. Our programs address the whole child — academics, emotional wellness, life skills, and character development.

As a federally recognized 501(c)(3) organization (EIN: 47-1775962), all donations to SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy are tax-deductible to the fullest extent allowed by law. We are committed to transparency, accountability, and maximizing the impact of every dollar we receive.

Our Story

From a Dream to a Movement

SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy was born from a deep conviction: that every child in our community deserves a champion. Our founders witnessed firsthand the devastating impact of educational inequity — children falling behind not due to lack of ability or ambition, but due to lack of access and support.

In the early days, we operated out of a single room at a local church in Shreveport's Allendale neighborhood, serving just 15 students with borrowed tables and donated textbooks. The demand was overwhelming. Parents lined up hoping for a spot. Teachers volunteered their evenings. The community rallied around a simple belief: education changes lives.

Over the years, SIHAF has grown dramatically. We expanded our programs, hired dedicated staff, trained volunteer mentors, and built partnerships with schools, businesses, and government agencies. Today, we serve over 1,200 students annually across multiple programs and locations throughout the Shreveport area.

Our journey is far from over. Every year, we deepen our impact and expand our reach. Every child we serve reminds us why we started — and why we will never stop.

What We Do

Our Programs & Services

Six comprehensive programs designed to support every K-12 student on their journey from classroom to career.

Program 1

After-School Academic Tutoring

Our flagship program provides free, high-quality tutoring in core academic subjects for students in grades K through 12. Every session is led by trained volunteer educators and supervised by certified teachers. Students receive individualized attention based on their current grade level, learning style, and academic goals.

We offer both one-on-one and small group tutoring formats, homework help, test preparation support, and reading intervention services for younger students. Our tutors are trained in culturally responsive teaching and trauma-informed practices to ensure every student feels safe, seen, and supported.

📅 Mon–Fri, 3–6 PM
👥 450+ Active Students
🆓 Completely Free
📍 350 Hearne Ave

Program 2

STEM Enrichment Lab

Our STEM Enrichment Lab brings the excitement of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics to life through hands-on, project-based learning. Students in grades 3–12 engage in experiments, robotics projects, coding workshops, and engineering challenges that build critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

The lab is equipped with computers, 3D printers, robotics kits, science equipment, and interactive learning materials. We partner with local businesses and universities to bring guest instructors, field trips, and real-world career connections to our students.

📅 Saturdays, 9 AM–1 PM
👥 180+ Students
🏆 Award-Winning Curriculum
🔬 Grades 3–12

Program 3

Youth Mentorship Program

The SIHAF Youth Mentorship Program connects students ages 10–18 with caring, trained adult mentors from across the Shreveport community. Mentors meet with their mentees biweekly for one-on-one sessions focused on academic goal setting, social-emotional skill building, career exploration, and character development.

Mentors come from diverse professional backgrounds including healthcare, law, engineering, education, entrepreneurship, and public service. Each mentor-mentee relationship is carefully matched and supported by our professional program staff.

📅 Biweekly Meetings
👥 200+ Student-Mentor Pairs
🎓 Ages 10–18
⭐ 95% Retention Rate

Program 4

College Readiness & Prep

Designed for students in grades 9–12, our College Readiness program demystifies the college application process and ensures every student has equal access to higher education opportunities. Services include SAT/ACT preparation, college essay writing workshops, financial aid guidance, scholarship searches, and college visits.

Our college counselors work individually with each student to build a personalized post-secondary plan that aligns with their talents, interests, and goals — whether that path leads to a four-year university, community college, vocational training, or the workforce.

🎓 Grades 9–12
📝 SAT/ACT Prep Included
💰 $480K+ Scholarships Found
🏫 89% College Acceptance Rate

Program 5

Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)

Academic success begins with emotional health. Our SEL program provides students with essential life skills including self-awareness, conflict resolution, emotional regulation, empathy, and resilience. Weekly group sessions and individual check-ins help students navigate the social and emotional challenges of adolescence with confidence and grace.

Our SEL facilitators are licensed social workers and mental health professionals who create safe, confidential spaces for students to process their feelings, build healthy relationships, and develop the social competencies needed to thrive both inside and outside the classroom.

📅 Weekly Sessions
👩‍⚕️ Licensed Facilitators
🧠 All Grade Levels
🔒 Confidential
🧠
Program 6

Summer Learning Camp

Our Summer Learning Camp prevents the "summer slide" — the well-documented loss of academic skills during school breaks — by providing an engaging, six-week summer enrichment experience for K-8 students. Campers participate in academic instruction, STEM activities, arts and crafts, field trips, sports, and team-building activities.

The camp is designed to be fun, culturally enriching, and academically rigorous. We provide breakfast and lunch daily, transportation assistance, and take-home activity kits. The camp is offered at low or no cost to families demonstrating financial need.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy, our programs, donations, and volunteering.

📚 About the Organization

What is SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy? ▼

SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy is a federally recognized 501(c)(3) nonprofit educational organization based in Shreveport, Louisiana. We provide free and low-cost academic support, mentorship, STEM enrichment, college readiness, and social-emotional learning programs to K-12 students in underserved communities.

Is SIHAF a 501(c)(3) nonprofit? ▼

Yes. SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy is a federally tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. Our Employer Identification Number (EIN) is 47-1775962. Donations to SIHAF are tax-deductible to the fullest extent allowed by law.

Where is SIHAF located? ▼

Our main office and program center is located at 350 Hearne Ave, Shreveport, LA 71103, United States. You can reach us by email at edu@sihafk12learningacademy.site. Our office is open Monday–Friday, 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM, and Saturdays, 9:00 AM – 2:00 PM.

When was SIHAF founded? ▼

SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy was founded in 2016 by a group of dedicated educators and community leaders in Shreveport, Louisiana. We received official IRS 501(c)(3) recognition in 2017 and have been serving students ever since.

🎓 Programs & Enrollment

Who can enroll in SIHAF programs? ▼

Our programs are open to all K-12 students in the Shreveport-Bossier City area. We prioritize enrollment for students from low-income households and underserved communities, but we welcome all students regardless of background. There are no academic requirements for enrollment.

Are your programs free? ▼

Most of our programs are completely free for eligible families. The Summer Learning Camp has a small nominal fee for families who can afford it, but no student is ever turned away due to inability to pay. We offer full financial assistance to qualifying families. Contact us to learn more about eligibility.

How do I enroll my child? ▼

To enroll your child, please visit our Contact page and submit an inquiry, or email us at edu@sihafk12learningacademy.site. A member of our team will reach out within 48 hours to discuss available programs, schedule an assessment, and guide you through the enrollment process.

What subjects do you tutor? ▼

We provide tutoring support in all core K-12 academic subjects, including Mathematics (arithmetic through calculus), English Language Arts and reading, Science (biology, chemistry, physics, earth science), Social Studies, and standardized test preparation for ACT, SAT, and state assessments.

❤️ Donations

How can I donate to SIHAF? ▼

You can donate online through our secure Donate page, by mailing a check to 350 Hearne Ave, Shreveport, LA 71103 (payable to "SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy"), or by contacting us at edu@sihafk12learningacademy.site to discuss corporate gifts, in-kind donations, or recurring contributions.

Is my donation tax-deductible? ▼

Yes. SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization (EIN: 47-1775962). All charitable donations are tax-deductible to the fullest extent permitted by law. You will receive a donation acknowledgment letter for your records, which serves as your tax receipt.

How are donations used? ▼

72% of every dollar goes directly to program delivery including tutoring, STEM education, mentorship, and college readiness. 15% supports community outreach and family engagement. 8% covers administrative operations, and 5% goes toward fundraising and development activities.

Can I make a recurring monthly donation? ▼

Absolutely! Monthly giving is one of the best ways to support SIHAF. Recurring donors provide us with reliable funding that allows us to plan programs, hire staff, and serve more students. You can set up a monthly gift on our Donate page and cancel or modify it at any time.

Can I get a refund on my donation? ▼

Due to the nature of charitable giving, donations are generally non-refundable. However, if a donation was made in error (e.g., wrong amount or duplicate transaction), please contact us within 30 days at edu@sihafk12learningacademy.site and we will work with you to resolve the issue.

🤝 Volunteering

Do I need a teaching degree to volunteer? ▼

No formal teaching degree is required for most volunteer positions. We welcome professionals, college students, retirees, and community members from all backgrounds. What matters most is subject knowledge, a genuine love for young people, and a commitment to showing up consistently.

Is there a background check requirement? ▼

Yes. All volunteers who work directly with students must pass a background check. We cover the cost of the background check for all SIHAF volunteers. This requirement is in place to ensure the safety and well-being of every student in our programs.

How do I sign up to volunteer? ▼

Visit our Volunteer page and complete the online interest form. A member of our team will contact you within 48 hours to discuss your interests, schedule, and the best volunteer role for you. From there, we'll guide you through orientation and background check procedures.

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website.   • Accessibility cookies: Remember your accessibility preferences such as font size and contrast settings.

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often, how long visitors spend on each page, and any error messages. This information is used to improve the performance of our website. All data collected is aggregated and anonymous.

Accessibility Statement

SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy's commitment to digital accessibility for all users

Effective Date: January 1, 2024  |  Last Updated: March 1, 2025

SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people of all abilities, including people with disabilities. We continually improve the user experience for everyone and apply the relevant accessibility standards.

1. Our Commitment

SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy believes that every person — regardless of ability, disability, age, or technological proficiency — deserves equal access to information about our programs, services, and mission. We are actively working to make our website accessible in accordance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA, as published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C).

2. Conformance Status

We aim to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards. Our ongoing efforts include:

  • Text Alternatives: Providing meaningful alt text for all images, icons, and non-text content.
  • Color Contrast: Ensuring sufficient color contrast ratios between text and backgrounds throughout the site.
  • Keyboard Navigation: Ensuring all functionality is accessible via keyboard navigation without requiring a mouse.
  • Readable Fonts: Using legible, scalable fonts that remain readable when zoomed or enlarged.
  • Clear Headings: Organizing content with logical heading hierarchies for screen reader users.
  • Form Labels: Providing clear labels and error messages for all form fields.
  • Language Declaration: Properly declaring the language of our web pages for assistive technology.
  • Focus Indicators: Maintaining visible focus indicators for keyboard users navigating through interactive elements.

3. Assistive Technology Compatibility

Our website is designed and tested for compatibility with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers including JAWS, NVDA, and VoiceOver
  • Voice recognition software such as Dragon NaturallySpeaking
  • Screen magnification software
  • Alternative input devices including keyboard-only navigation
  • High contrast display modes

4. Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, some content may not yet fully conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Known areas we are actively working to improve include:

  • Some older PDF documents may not be fully accessible to screen readers. We are working to remediate or replace these documents.
  • Some embedded third-party content (such as maps and social media feeds) may not be fully controlled by us and may have their own accessibility limitations.
  • Videos and multimedia content are being progressively updated to include captions and transcripts.

5. Accessibility Features of Our Website

  • 🌙 Dark Mode: Toggle dark mode for reduced eye strain using the moon icon in the navigation bar.
  • 🔍 Scalable Text: All text can be enlarged using browser zoom functions without loss of content or functionality.
  • ⌨️ Keyboard Accessible: All major features of our website are navigable via keyboard.
  • 📱 Mobile Responsive: Our website is fully responsive and accessible on all devices and screen sizes.

6. Feedback & Contact

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of our website. If you experience any barriers, have difficulty accessing any content, or need information in an alternative format, please contact us:

In the United States, accessibility complaints related to organizations receiving federal funding may be directed to the U.S. Department of Education's Office for Civil Rights.

8. Assessment Approach

SIHAF K-12 Learning Academy assesses the accessibility of our website through:
